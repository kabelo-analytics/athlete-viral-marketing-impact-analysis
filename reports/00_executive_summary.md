# Executive Summary (Recruiter-friendly)

## Context
This project simulates a modern marketing reality: **one viral moment** can outperform months of traditional advertising — but only if it converts into real outcomes.

I designed a synthetic dataset around an **extreme athlete-led campaign** and measured:
- reach + engagement (top of funnel),
- conversions (signups → trials → memberships),
- brand lift (search interest proxy),
- offline behaviour (gym visits + new memberships proxy).

## What I found (in this synthetic scenario)
- TikTok drove the biggest reach and buzz, but it wasn’t always the most efficient conversion channel.
- YouTube tended to perform better on cost per membership once the longer-form trailer content hit.
- The Website converted best (as expected) — it’s where intent becomes action.
- Brand interest spiked around the stunt moment and stayed elevated through the campaign.
- Gym activity showed a lagged uplift (behaviour change isn’t instant).

## Recommendations
- Keep TikTok as the awareness engine, but pair it with a clean next step (landing page + offer).
- Use YouTube as the “trust layer” (longer-form narrative content that converts).
- Treat the Website as the conversion hub; optimise it weekly.
- Measure impact week-over-week, not only day-by-day (because wellness behaviour has lag).
