# Executive Summary (Recruiter-friendly)

## Context
This project simulates a modern marketing reality: one viral moment can outperform months of traditional advertising, but only if it converts into real outcomes.

I designed a synthetic dataset around an extreme athlete-led campaign and measured:
- reach + engagement (top of funnel),
- conversions (signups -> trials -> memberships),
- brand lift (search interest proxy),
- offline behaviour (gym visits + new memberships proxy).

## What I found (in this synthetic scenario)
- TikTok drove the biggest reach and buzz, but it was not the strongest downstream conversion channel on a per-click basis.
- Among spend-bearing channels, TikTok carried the lowest reported CPA in this dataset, while Instagram and YouTube converted clicks more efficiently downstream.
- The Website converted best as a destination touchpoint, but it should not be interpreted as a comparable paid or earned media channel.
- Brand interest spiked during the campaign window and remained above the pre-campaign average afterward.
- Gym membership activity jumped around campaign launch and then eased back rather than following a clean fixed-lag pattern.

## Recommendations
- Keep TikTok as the awareness engine, but pair it with a clean next step (landing page + offer).
- Separate media efficiency reporting from Website conversion reporting so the scorecard does not mix channels with destinations.
- Treat the Website as the conversion hub; optimise landing-page clarity, CTA prominence, and offer handoff weekly.
- Monitor offline demand most closely during launch week and immediately after peak campaign moments.
