# Insights & Recommendations

This analysis is most useful when it helps a marketer decide what to do next, not just what happened.

## 1) Viral performance was concentrated around the live campaign window
Reach and demand moved sharply around the campaign launch rather than evenly across the period.
That is why this project needs both a funnel view and a timing view, not just a "views" chart.

## 2) TikTok drove scale, but not the strongest downstream conversion
It dominated reach and generated the largest absolute membership volume.
But its memberships per click still trailed Website, Instagram, and YouTube.
In practice, TikTok should be judged on scale, downstream handoff quality, and total contribution rather than on one efficiency metric alone.

## 3) Conversion destinations should be read separately from media
The Website converted better because it is where intent becomes action.
That does not mean the Website is the best marketing channel.
It means reporting should separate media acquisition from destination conversion.

## 4) Offline response concentrated around launch
In this synthetic dataset, gym activity and new memberships spiked early in the campaign window, then normalised.
That is a useful timing signal, but it is not evidence of a clean two-week offline lag.

## Action plan
- TikTok for attention and scale, with landing-page handoff tracked tightly.
- Website for conversion (single CTA, fast page, clear offer).
- Separate paid/earned channel CPA reporting from Website conversion reporting.
- Monitor offline membership demand during launch week and immediately after peak campaign moments.
- Track cost per membership, conversion rates, and offline behaviour together rather than using clicks alone.
